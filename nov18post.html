My Picks: Sunday, November 18th

Posted by Sam Worrod on November 18, 2018

Last Night: 6-4

N/A

Game 2: New York Knicks at Orlando Magic

My Pick:

Orlando Magic

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Orlando Magic have been very up and down so far, and have continued to be one of the teams that I can't get a read on. That said, Vucevic has been playing extremely well lately, and has looked to be a driving force for this Magic team. Count on him to put up numbers against New York, whose interior defense is sorely lacking. Both teams don't have offensively elite point guards, so it will be interesting to see what happens there. Magic, 112-108.

Vucevic has been playing great for Orlando so far this season.

Game 3: Los Angeles Lakers at Miami Heat

My Pick:

Los Angeles Lakers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Lakers looked rough against the Magic last night, but part of that could be the light minutes Lebron played. Part of it could also be attributed to Lonzo being held scoreless, and the second option of Ingram being held to just 17 points. Javale McGee also played limited minutes, and could be another factor tonight against the athletic bigs of Miami. Hart on Richardson and Ball on Dragic should be a good perimeter battle, but Lebron takes them over the edge as the Lakers win, 109-104.

Game 4: Portland Trail Blazers at Washington Wizards

My Pick:

Portland Trail Blazers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

Dwight Howard was probably the Wizards best player in their most recent game, which says a lot about this team which also has Wall and Beal on it. I'm still low on the Wizards, and I don't see that changing. The Portland backcourt is just better, and as a collective I would say the rest of the Portland lineup is better too. Because of this, I'm comfortable saying that the Blazers will win, 117-107.

Game 5: Golden State Warriors at San Antonio Spurs

My Pick:

San Antonio Spurs

Why I'm Rolling with them:

This was a tough game for me to pick, but the Golden State Warriors have actually looked vulnerable lately. They have a ton of players sitting out, including Steph Curry and Draymond Green. That should be enough for Pop and the Spurs to steal this one at home against a tired Golden State squad that has had a lot of real issues so far this season in terms of communication. San Antonio wins, 120-115.
