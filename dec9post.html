My Picks: Sunday, December 9th

Posted by Sam Worrod on December 9, 2018

Last Night: 3-6

Game 1: New Orleans Pelicans at Detroit Pistons

My Pick:

New Orleans Pelicans

Why I'm Rolling with them:

Drummond is the weaker of the two centers here, which can't be said in about 80% of the games the Pistons play. Davis is a force of nature, and should be more than capable of dominating the matchup, even if Drummond still puts up good numbers. The real question is whether or not the supporting cast will be enough to help him. In this case, I think it will be. Pelicans, 119-114.

Game 2: Milwaukee Bucks at Toronto Raptors

My Pick:

Toronto Raptors

Why I'm Rolling with them:

This game is hard for me to pick since the Raptors have looked a lot worse lately, shooting absolutely horribly from 3 and having abysmal rebounding numbers. Yet somehow, even with all that they've stayed atop the Eastern Conference standings, with a convincing win over the 76ers before losing in a shocker to the Nets. The Bucks have been pretty mediocre since their 7-0 start, going 9-7 since. Both teams have definite weaknesses, and Middleton has been in a slump as of late. The difference maker in this game could be whichever team decides to show up. My money is on the Raptors, 117-110.

Game 3: Utah Jazz at San Antonio Spurs

My Pick:

San Antonio Spurs

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Spurs recently had a great game against the Lakers in which they bounced back and their offense seemed to actually click. At the same time, their defense still leaves a lot to be desired. Luckily, the Jazz aren't some offensive powerhouse that will be able to capitalize on that, so the Spurs SHOULD be safe for tonight. This team has the potential to be far better than they are, and if the pieces start clicking that could be very scary. Spurs, 120-115.

Game 4: Charlotte Hornets at New York Knicks

My Pick:

Charlotte Hornets

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Hornets are just better than the Knicks. When you take that and compound it with the fact that the Knicks played yesterday, this isn't looking good for them. The Hornets have the best player on the court in Kemba Walker, who has continued to delight so far this year with a very good scoring season, propelling this Hornets team to the Seven seed. No one on NY will be able to stop him and it will show. Hornets win this one 118-110.
