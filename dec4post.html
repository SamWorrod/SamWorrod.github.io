My Picks: Tuesday, December 4th

Posted by Sam Worrod on December 4, 2018

Saturday: 4-3

Game 1: Chicago Bulls at Indiana Pacers

My Pick:

Indiana Pacers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

Even with Oladipo still out, the Pacers should be able to make quick work of the Bulls, who are currently sitting at tied for last place with a 5-19 record. The Pacers have been playing good basketball, and their 1 point loss to the Kings on Saturday should fuel them in a win tonight. Look to see Bogdanovic make an impact in one of the forward spots, although Markkanen being back may derail his effectiveness slightly. Pacers take this one 110-102.

Game 2: Orlando Magic at Miami Heat

My Pick:

Orlando Magic

Why I'm Rolling with them:

One of the more suprising storylines for me this season is that the Magic are occupying a playoff spot and are 1.5 games up on the team in 9th. Miami on the other hand has struggled, and are sitting at 9-13 after wins over the Pelicans and Jazz. The Magic should be able to use Vucevic's range to pull Whiteside out onto the perimeter, where his ability to rebound will be hampered by his distance from the basket. Orlando win a close game 107-103.

Game 3: Portland Trail Blazers at Dallas Mavericks

My Pick:

Portland Trail Blazers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Portland Trail Blazers have continued their excellent start, with a 13-10 record so far this season. That may not seem like much, but that's only 3 games back from first in the West, and should continue to let them occupy a playoff spot. Lillard is coming off of an excellent performance on Sunday in a loss to the Spurs, that saw the bench put up only 13 combined points. This should cement once again that this is a starter oriented team, but those starters should be enough to hold off the Mavericks tonight. Blazers take it 118-114.

Game 4: San Antonio Spurs at Utah Jazz

My Pick:

San Antonio Spurs

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Jazz and the Spurs are two of the middle of the pack teams in the west, seperated by a single loss. This game should be close either way, but I think the Jazz have still looked shaky lately and DeMar will have the upper hand over Mitchell, who still needs to work on his shot creation this season. In addition, Gobert works better when the star opposing big operates in the paint, and LMA is a master of the midrange. Spurs win this one 108-102.

Game 5: Sacramento Kings at Phoenix Suns

My Pick:

Sacramento Kings

Why I'm Rolling with them:

Devin Booker is out tonight, and with it goes Phoenix's chances of having a close game against Sacramento. The Kings are the superior team by far once you remove the Sun's primary ball handler and playmaker in Booker, and Fox has the speed necessary to demolish whoever Phoenix chooses to put on him. Ayton is still a little too raw to carry an offense by himself, which is why I think the Suns will struggle on that end. Kings, 112-96.
