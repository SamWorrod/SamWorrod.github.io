My Picks: Thursday, December 13th

Posted by Sam Worrod on December 13, 2018

Last Night: 5-6

Game 1: Los Angeles Lakers at Houston Rockets

My Pick:

Los Angeles Lakers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Rockets don't have any answer for Lebron, unless you consider "Lebron Stopper" PJ Tucker, who is not by any means a Lebron stopper. They have athletic Centers to deal with Capela, and excellent perimeter defenders in Kuzma and Lonzo, who will be able to at least slow down Harden and Paul. The rest of the Rockets Roster is absolutely abyssmal. I don't see them coming close, as the Lakers win 119-109.

Game 2: Los Angeles Clippers at San Antonio Spurs

My Pick:

San Antonio Spurs

Why I'm Rolling with them:

This game should be very close, and I think that the mismatches here revolve around the guard positions. I could see Gilgeous-Alexander being a tough defender for DeRozan, but I think that the Spurs veteran is a little too crafty and will be able to put his defender in foul trouble. Forbes has also been playing incredibly well, and scored 24 last night in a win over the Suns. None of their starters played much, and should be good for a 118-110 win.

Game 3: Chicago Bulls at Orlando Magic

My Pick:

Orlando Magic

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Magic are the better team here, and the Bulls have a lot more to worry about than whether or not LaVine and Markkanen can mesh. They've gone to the NBA Players Association, complaining that their coach is making them practice on the day after a back to back. Players that don't even want to practice, and a coach that doesn't want to listen to their players is a sign of a disfunctional organization. Magic take this one 120-108.

Game 4: Dallas Mavericks at Phoenix Suns

My Pick:

Dallas Mavericks

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Dallas Mavericks should absolutely decimate the Phoenix Suns. Two days ago the Suns got absolutely shellshocked by the Spurs, a team that has also been struggling so far this year. The Mavericks have been doing incredibly well so far this season, and Jordan is a really nice matchup against Ayton. Doncic is basically a 3/4 who plays point, and allows the Mavericks to have shooters in the 1 and 2 spots, which should help against Phoenix who has had trouble guarding quicker perimeter players. Dallas, 113-96.
