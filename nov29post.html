My Picks: Thursday, November 29th

Posted by Sam Worrod on November 29, 2018

Last Night: 10-0

Game 1: Golden State Warriors at Toronto Raptors

My Pick:

Toronto Raptors

Why I'm Rolling with them:

I feel as though we have been robbed of a great game tonight. Steph Curry and Draymond Green are out tonight, meaning that Golden State will be forced to rely on.... Kevin Durant and Klay Thompson, two of the best shooters to ever play the game. This should still be a semi-challenging game for the Raptors, but they have two of the best wing defenders in the league in the form of Green and Leonard, who should be able to at least contain the Warriors all-stars enough to win this one 112-108.

Game 2: Los Angeles Lakers at Indiana Pacers

My Pick:

Los Angeles Lakers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Los Angeles Lakers are on the road tonight in Indiana, where Lebron makes his return after beating them in 7 during the first round of the 2018 playoffs. Brandon Ingram and Lebron have yet to fully gel on the court, but this is as good a game as any for it to happen. The Pacers are without Oladipo once again, and I am not sure that they'll be able to play the same hard-nosed defense that the Cavs struggled against last year. Lebron will have a great game, and Ingram will bounce back in a 115-108 win.

The Laker's 11-9 start is the worst start for a Lebron team since 2007

Game 3: Los Angeles Clippers at Sacramento Kings

My Pick:

Sacramento Kings

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Sacramento Kings are one of my favourite teams to watch in the league. DeAaron Fox has consistently been one of the more fun players to follow, and I am confident he'll be able to have another good game again tonight against Gilgeous-Alexander, a promising rookie who I think will be just a step slow tonight. In addition, the Kings have some decent players off the bench, which should help against the Clippers bench mob. My prediction is Fox and Hield lead the Kings to the win, 119-116.
