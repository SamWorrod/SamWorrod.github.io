My Picks: Tuesday, December 18th

Posted by Sam Worrod on December 18, 2018

Dec 13th: Record

Game 1: Cleveland Cavaliers at Indiana Pacers

My Pick:

Indiana Pacers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Cleveland Cavaliers have been struggling as of late, losing 2 of their last 3 since Tristan Thompson went down with an injury a week ago. Their one win came against the Knicks, who were on the tail end of a back to back, and that was a fairly close game. Now, they go up against the Pacers, rejuvenated since the return of Victor Oladipo, and on a mission to get a good seed in the East. I see no outcome where the Cavs take this one, the far more likely case is the Pacers destroying the boards, and Oladipo harrassing them on the perimeter. Pacers, 114-102.

Game 2: Los Angeles Lakers at Brooklyn Nets

My Pick:

Los Angeles Lakers

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Lakers got humiliated two nights ago at the hands of the Wizards, a team that shouldn't compare to the Lakers in terms of talent. The Nets also fall into that category, but have looked hot lately and ready to upset a good team. This one was actually fairly hard for me to call, but in the end I think Lebron will have a monster bounce back game that will result in the rest of the Lakers also showing out. Los Angeles takes this one 119-112.

Game 3: Washington Wizards at Atlanta Hawks

My Pick:

Washington Wizards

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Wizards have recently accquired Trevor Ariza, and he'll be starting for them tonight against Atlanta. I'm assuming he's playing the 4 for them, and Otto Porter will be starting at the three. The Wizards have the capability to be a dangerous first round opponent for a team. But first they'll have to get there, and games against teams like the Hawks are must wins for them. The Wizards win this one handily, 114-105.

Game 4: Dallas Mavericks at Denver Nuggets

My Pick:

Denver Nuggets

Why I'm Rolling with them:

The Nuggets currently sit atop the Western Conference Standings, tied with the Warriors for the top spot. Their team plays a great style of basketball where the ball is almost never held up with one person, inspired by the passing prowess of Jokic. I have faith that he'll be an All-Nba player this season, and may even take the first team spot over Anthony Davis, who is a better player but on a middling team. Nuggets 120-110.
